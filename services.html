Matte Paper Stock

Description: Matte paper has a non-reflective, flat surface that diffuses light, resulting in a softer appearance compared to glossy paper. It typically has a smooth texture but lacks the shine of glossy paper.
Advantages: Matte paper reduces glare and fingerprints, making it ideal for prints that will be handled frequently or viewed under direct lighting. It also provides a more subtle, understated look compared to glossy paper.
Common Uses: Art prints, posters, fine art reproductions, text-heavy documents, and any application where reduced glare and a more subdued finish are desired.

Glossy Paper Stock

Description: Glossy paper has a shiny, reflective surface that enhances the vibrancy and depth of colors in printed images. It typically has a smooth texture.
Advantages: Glossy paper produces sharp, vibrant prints with excellent color saturation and contrast. It's ideal for printing photos and images that you want to showcase with a glossy finish.
Common Uses: Photography prints, promotional materials, brochures, flyers, and presentations where color accuracy and visual impact are important.

Textured Paper Stock

Description: Textured paper has a surface with a distinct texture, such as a canvas-like weave, linen-like pattern, or watercolor-like texture. The texture adds depth and character to prints, creating a unique tactile experience.
Advantages: Textured paper adds visual interest and a tactile element to prints, enhancing their artistic appeal. It can also help mimic the look and feel of traditional art mediums.
Common Uses: Fine art prints, invitations, greeting cards, stationery, and any project where you want to add a touch of sophistication or artistic flair.
